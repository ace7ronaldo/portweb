Unite to Divide, then Divide to Unite

Context

We had to select a faction and separate them from the existing community. This was supposed to be a satire of sorts. We had to create re-design their identity and portray through design the propaganda. The final design had to be presented on a poster.

Making

Nothing could have prepared me for the complexity of this project. Being an Indian I felt using a faction from India made more sense as I can identify with the faction a little better. I had to read and research on flag design, the community, etc. The more I learned the more complex it got for me to design the identity of the faction. To fulfill the satire requirement I wanted the project to have the essence of the show 'Black Mirror', which would require a technology element. I started by designing the flag which would embody the idea of unity despite the difference and acceptance of a new nation. Then I got started on the 'Black Mirror' aspect with the emblem and the means through which the propaganda was to be executed. I made emblems not only for the state but to identify the people as well, this task was quite tedious as I had to accommodate the various differences and simplify the complexities. For one of my classes, I did a group project where we used A-Frame to create a virtual tour of the NC state graphic design graduate program. While working on the project I enjoyed working on it, so I decided to use it to create a mockup of a news app through which the propaganda would be implemented. It wasn't as simple as I had expected, deciding the position of the elements, deciding the content, how best to subtly project the propaganda, given the limited time I had. This project gave me a sense of the power of design and the responsibility a designer possess.
